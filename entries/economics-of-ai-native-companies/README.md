# The economics of AI-native companies

Economics pillar anchor. Traces the structural shift in what it takes to start a software company: old economics (ten to fifteen people, multi-million seed, twelve to eighteen month burn), new economics (one to three people, near-zero capital, weeks to revenue), the long tail of newly viable small products, concrete unit economics at $10K MRR, and the narrow case for funding.

~2,400 words. References Vol XI (End of Subsidized AI).
